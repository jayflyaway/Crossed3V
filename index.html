Crossed-3 and Crossed 3V

August 2021

The Crossed 3 is another pattern of the Crossed V-Variations. Like the Crossed V , it is based on a crossed walkway, where the juggler walking over to change the feeder now walks through the pattern to the other side, instead of around the outside. Characteristically for all Crossed V Variation pattern is that the hands are flipped after every transition.

Fig 1. Crossed walkway. Positions are relabeled and marked as (\(⋅\))' to determinate the mirrored version, where all throws and manipulation happens on the left hand.

While it is quite hard to juggle during the transition when walking through the pattern, the Crossed 3 come with a charming "attribute". The normally walking B is intercepted by the Manipulator. Which causes that the walk through the pattern is replaced by the manipulation.

\(B\) \(P_A\) \(S\) \(S\) \(S\) \(P_A\) \(S\) \(S\) \(\rightarrow C'\)
\(C\) \(S \) \(S \) \(P_A \) \(S \) \(S \) \(S \) \(S\) \(\rightarrow A'\)
\(M\) \(C^C_C \) - \(S_C^A\) - \(I_B \) - - \(\rightarrow M'\)

Similar to the Crossed V, the manipulation sequence follows the one of the Scrambled-V-Variations. However due to the change of the handedness, one will have a certain role only with on the right or on the left handed side, never on both.

As shown above in the table, \(J_1 \equiv J_3\) and \(J_2 \equiv J_4\) according to their permutation.

Combining Crossed V and Crossed 3

As shown by Lukas [2] any two different Scrambled V-Variations can be combined by adding another manipulation \(N\) into the pattern. This can likewise be done with the Crossed V and Crossed 3.
